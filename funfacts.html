Fun Facts About Me

A Day on Venus is Longer Than Its Year

Venus takes 243 Earth days to rotate once, but only about 225 Earth days to orbit the Sun

Email is Older Than the World Wide Web

Electronic mail was first developed in the early 1970s for ARPANET (the precursor to the internet). The World Wide Web, which uses HTTP and browsers, wasn't invented until 1989.

The First Computer Mouse Was Wooden

The original computer mouse, invented by Douglas Engelbart in 1964, was a bulky, hand-carved wooden shell with a cord resembling a tail

Ada Lovelace Was the First Programmer

In the mid-1800s, Ada Lovelace wrote the world's first computer program for Charles Babbage's conceptual Analytical Engine

The "Bug" Was a Real Moth

She taped the insect into the logbook, coining the term debugging.

QWERTY Slows You Down

The QWERTY layout was designed for early typewriters to prevent mechanical keys from jamming by slowing the typist

Google's Binary Tweet: Google's f

Google's Binary Tweet: Google's first tweet in 2009 was "I'm feeling lucky" but written entirely in binary code

One-Row Word

The longest word you can type using only the top row of a QWERTY keyboard is "typewriter"
